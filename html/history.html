History

As Sikhs began to realize the oppressive regimes of the Indian government, this including the erasure of our mother tongue (Punjabi), being mislabeled and treated as second class citizens, drugs being poured into our homeland to suppress us, corruption amongst government with killings or torture of Sikhs by the hands of police in fake encounters… A Saintly Warrior emerged in this era named Sant Jarnail Singh Bhinderanwale. He fought vigorously for the rights of Sikh’s and started to arm the youth against this oppression. Long in the making was the plan of eliminating this movement and attacking the heart of the Sikh faith. In June 1984, Prime Minister Indira Gandhi ordered Operation Bluestar which was an army invasion of Harmandir Sahib (Sikh’s holiest shrine). Her goal was to kill off all innocent Sikh’s that supported the movement of Sikh rights and labeling them as terrorists for retaliating against the government’s tyranny. While this battle took place there was a media blackout. Punjab (the homeland of Sikhs) was cut off from the rest of the world, leaving the government to get away with murder and injustice.
